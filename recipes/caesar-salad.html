Caesar Salad

Description

A Caesar salad (also spelled Cesar, César and Cesare) is a green salad of romaine lettuce and croutons dressed with lemon juice (or lime juice), olive oil, egg, Worcestershire sauce, anchovies, garlic, Dijon mustard, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper. In its original form, this salad was prepared and served tableside.

Ingredients

  • Romaine lettuce
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Baguette
  • Lemon juice
  • Olive oil
  • Eggs
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Anchovies
  • Garlic
  • Dijon mustard
  • Black pepper

Instructions

  1. Cut baguette in half lengthwise and slice into 1/4″ thick pieces and place on baking sheet.
  2. Combine 3 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil with minced garlic. toss breads with garlic infused oil and 2 Tbsp parmesan.
  3. Spread Evenly and Bake to desired crispness.
  4. Whisk together minced garlic, dijon, Worcestershire, lemon juice and red wine vinegar.
  5. Whisking while adding oil emulsifies the dressing for a smooth and creamy (not oily) consistency.
  6. Season with 1/2 tsp salt and 1/8 tsp black pepper, or to taste.
  7. In a large mixing bowl, combine all of your ingredients and toss gently to coat the lettuce in caesar dressing. This recipe makes enough croutons for two full salads so you'll have them ready to go for round 2!
